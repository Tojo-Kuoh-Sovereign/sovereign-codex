# Colour Palette

The Colour Palette defines the materials, colours, and semantic roles that establish the visual language of Sovereign Codex.

Rather than selecting colours for decoration, the palette is derived from materials that represent the values, permanence, and craftsmanship of the platform.

Every colour should reinforce trust, clarity, and hierarchy while remaining comfortable for long-form reading.

---

## Purpose

The Colour Palette exists to provide a consistent visual foundation across the entire platform.

Colours should communicate meaning before aesthetics and support the identity established by the Design Philosophy.

The palette should remain restrained, timeless, and purposeful.

---

## Colour Philosophy

Sovereign Codex begins with materials rather than colours.

Each material represents a characteristic of the platform and is translated into a digital colour palette.

The interface becomes a modern interpretation of enduring materials rather than an arbitrary collection of colours.

---

## Material Palette

| Material | Represents | Typical Usage |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| **Obsidian** | Foundation, permanence | Primary backgrounds |
| **Forged Steel** | Structure, engineering | Borders, navigation, secondary surfaces |
| **Bronze** | Stewardship, craftsmanship | Headings, accents, emphasis |
| **Sapphire** | Knowledge, trust | Interactive elements, links, active navigation |
| **Paper** | Human knowledge | Primary text and readable content |

Each material contributes to the overall character of the platform while serving a clear functional purpose.

---

## Semantic Colour System

Colours should be referenced by their semantic role rather than their visual appearance.

Examples include:

- Surface
- Border
- Text
- Heading
- Interactive
- Success
- Warning
- Critical

This approach improves maintainability while allowing the visual identity to evolve without changing implementation.

---

## Hierarchy

Colour should establish hierarchy before attracting attention.

Primary information should always remain the visual focus.

Accent colours should guide the reader rather than dominate the interface.

Restraint creates clarity.

---

## Accessibility

The colour palette should support comfortable long-form reading.

Contrast should remain sufficient for readability while avoiding unnecessary visual fatigue.

Colour should never be the sole method of communicating information.

---

## Evolution

The palette should evolve cautiously.

New colours should only be introduced when they communicate new meaning rather than additional decoration.

Consistency strengthens recognition over time.

---

## Continuous Improvement

The Colour Palette should evolve only when doing so strengthens the clarity, accessibility, or long-term identity of Sovereign Codex.

Every addition should reinforce the existing design language rather than compete with it.
