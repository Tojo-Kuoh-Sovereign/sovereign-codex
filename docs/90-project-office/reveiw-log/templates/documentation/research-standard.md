# Research Standard

The Research Standard defines how information should be documented while it is being investigated within Sovereign Codex.

Its purpose is to encourage structured investigation, preserve supporting evidence, and clearly distinguish research from verified knowledge.

Research documentation should prioritise transparency and repeatability over certainty.

---

## Core Principles

Research should be:

- Structured
- Transparent
- Repeatable
- Evidence-based
- Open to revision

---

## Purpose

Research documents exist to explore, investigate, and evaluate information that has not yet reached the project's standard for publication within the Knowledge section.

Research should capture observations, assumptions, testing, and supporting evidence without presenting unverified conclusions as established fact.

---

## Research Structure

Where appropriate, research documentation should include:

- Research objective
- Background information
- Methodology
- Observations
- Supporting evidence
- Assumptions
- Conclusions
- Outstanding questions

Not every investigation will require every section.

Documentation should remain appropriate to the scope of the research.

---

## Evidence

Research should reference supporting evidence wherever practical.

Evidence should follow the project's Citation Standard and be evaluated using the Evidence Model.

Supporting material should remain clearly distinguishable from personal interpretation.

---

## Assumptions

Assumptions should be clearly identified.

Where assumptions influence conclusions, they should remain open to review as additional evidence becomes available.

Research should never present assumptions as established fact.

---

## Transition to Knowledge

Research becomes eligible for publication within the Knowledge section once it satisfies the project's governance, review, and evidence requirements.

Publication should represent the current best understanding of the subject rather than the conclusion of all future investigation.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Research standards should evolve alongside the project.

Improvements should strengthen transparency, repeatability, and the long-term quality of published knowledge while preserving flexibility for future investigation.
