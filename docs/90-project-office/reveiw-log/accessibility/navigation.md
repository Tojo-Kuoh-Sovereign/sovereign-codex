Research Note — Navigation & Wayfinding
Current Observation

Navigation & Wayfinding appears to be a topic where evidence is likely to define requirements rather than prescribe a single implementation.

The research is expected to explain why people need orientation, contextual awareness, and guidance while navigating knowledge. However, the specific mechanisms used to satisfy those needs may vary between knowledge systems.

Implementation should therefore remain open until sufficient evidence has been gathered and practical experimentation within the Sovereign Codex has taken place.

Current Understanding

The objective of Navigation & Wayfinding is not simply to help users move between pages.

It is to help users:

Understand where they are.
Understand how information is related.
Maintain orientation.
Navigate towards relevant knowledge with confidence.
Explore additional knowledge without becoming disoriented.
Research Considerations

Current implementation ideas (such as breadcrumbs, inline links, contextual recommendations, knowledge pathways, or guided progression) should be treated as hypotheses, not conclusions.

Research should investigate:

Why existing navigation patterns are effective.
When different navigation methods are appropriate.
Whether effectiveness changes depending on a user's Knowledge State.
How navigation can encourage exploration while preserving comprehension.
How navigation contributes to trust, orientation, and long-term understanding.
Design Observation

The Sovereign Codex may ultimately develop a distinctive approach to Navigation & Wayfinding that reflects its identity as a knowledge system rather than a traditional wiki.

However, this should emerge from validated principles rather than personal preference.

The objective is not to create something different for its own sake, but to express evidence-based principles in a way that aligns with the Sovereign vision.

Governance Note

Navigation & Wayfinding is likely to become a significant area of practical experimentation during implementation.

Prototype pages, user observation, and iterative refinement may provide valuable implementation feedback once the underlying principles have been established.
