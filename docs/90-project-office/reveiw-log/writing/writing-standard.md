# Writing Standard

The Writing Standard establishes the conventions used when creating documentation within Sovereign Codex.

Its purpose is to ensure documentation remains clear, consistent, and easy to understand regardless of subject matter or contributor.

Rather than prescribing a particular writing style, this standard promotes communication that is accurate, practical, and focused on the reader.

---

## Core Principles

Documentation should be:

- Clear
- Concise
- Consistent
- Objective
- Reader-focused

These principles apply to all written content within the project.

---

## Clarity

Write using plain language wherever possible.

Complex topics should be explained clearly without introducing unnecessary technical complexity.

---

## Conciseness

Include enough information to support understanding without unnecessary repetition.

Every paragraph should contribute meaningful value to the reader.

---

## Consistency

Use consistent terminology, formatting, and tone throughout the project.

Similar concepts should be explained in similar ways.

---

## Objectivity

Documentation should present information factually.

Avoid unnecessary opinion, speculation, or subjective language unless clearly identified.

---

## Reader-First Design

Write for the reader, not the author.

Structure information so readers can quickly locate, understand, and apply what they need.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Writing standards should evolve through practical experience and ongoing review.

Improvements should increase clarity, consistency, and maintainability without unnecessarily increasing complexity.
