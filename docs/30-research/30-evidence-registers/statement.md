# Purpose

Evidence Registers provide a structured record of the evidence collected throughout research conducted within the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Their purpose is to organise observations, academic literature, industry guidance, implementation experience, and other credible sources in a transparent and traceable manner.

Evidence Registers support objective research by separating evidence collection from interpretation and conclusion.

---

# Statement

Evidence should be gathered before conclusions are reached.

The Sovereign Documentation Framework uses Evidence Registers to document the information considered during research while maintaining transparency regarding its origin, relevance, and reliability.

Evidence Registers do not establish principles or standards.

Instead, they provide the evidential foundation from which recurring concepts may be identified, validated, and eventually incorporated into Governance and Standards.

---

# Principles

## Evidence Before Interpretation

Evidence should be recorded independently of conclusions wherever practical.

Maintaining this separation reduces bias and improves transparency throughout the research process.

---

## Transparency

Evidence should identify its source wherever practical.

Readers should be able to understand where information originated and how it contributed to the research process.

---

## Diverse Sources

Evidence may be gathered from a variety of credible sources appropriate to the subject being investigated, including:

- Official game documentation and announcements
- In-game observation and testing
- Experimental results
- Community knowledge and discussion
- Established reference material
- Practical implementation and experience

The credibility and relevance of evidence should always be considered within the context of the research question.

No single source should normally determine a research outcome.

---

## Traceability

Evidence should remain traceable throughout the research lifecycle.

Research Concepts, Validated Concepts, Principles, and Standards should be capable of referencing the evidence that informed their development.

---

## Continuous Refinement

Evidence Registers are living documents.

As new evidence becomes available, registers should be reviewed and expanded to improve the quality and completeness of the research.

# Governance

Evidence Registers should remain objective, transparent, and evidence-informed.

The purpose of an Evidence Register is to document available evidence rather than advocate for particular conclusions or implementation decisions.

Interpretation should occur separately through Research Concepts and Validated Concepts.
