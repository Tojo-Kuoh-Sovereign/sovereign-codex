# Research

Research provides the evidence-informed foundation of the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Its purpose is to investigate meaningful questions, collect and evaluate evidence, identify recurring concepts, and validate findings that may inform Governance, Principles, Standards, and future implementation.

Research does not establish governance directly.

Instead, it provides the evidence from which governance decisions can be made with confidence.

---

## Research Structure

The Research section is organised into the following areas.

### Methodology

Defines how research is conducted throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

---

### Research Questions

Defines the questions that guide investigation.

---

### Evidence Registers

Documents the evidence gathered during research.

---

### Research Concepts

Captures recurring concepts that emerge from evidence.

---

### Validated Concepts

Records concepts that have demonstrated sufficient confidence to inform Governance and Standards.

---

## Research in Practice

Together these sections provide a structured process for transforming questions into trusted knowledge through evidence, validation, and continuous improvement.

Research supports Governance by providing an evidence-informed foundation for future Principles, Standards, and implementation decisions.
