# Purpose

The Research Methodology defines the structured process used to investigate, validate, and develop evidence-informed concepts throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

The methodology provides a repeatable workflow for transforming research into principles, standards, and implementation guidance while maintaining transparency, consistency, and continuous improvement.

Unlike the Research Philosophy, which defines *why* research is conducted, the Research Methodology defines *how* research is performed.

---

# Overview

Research within the Sovereign Documentation Framework follows an evidence-informed methodology designed to identify enduring concepts rather than collect individual opinions.

The objective is to build principles and standards that are supported by recurring evidence from multiple credible sources and validated through practical application.

Each stage of the methodology provides the foundation for the next.

---

# Research Workflow

Every research topic follows the same evidence-informed workflow.

```text
Research Question

↓

Evidence Register

↓

Evidence-Based Concept

↓

Validation

↓

Knowledge Presentation Principle

↓

Presentation Standard

↓

Implementation
```

Each stage must justify the next.

---

# Research Question

Research begins by defining a clear question.

The question establishes the scope of investigation and ensures that research remains focused on understanding rather than implementation.

---

# Evidence Register

Evidence is gathered from multiple independent and credible sources.

The objective is not to collect as many sources as possible, but to identify recurring concepts that consistently emerge across the available evidence.

Where conflicting evidence exists, it should be documented rather than ignored.

---

# Evidence-Based Concepts

Recurring observations are consolidated into candidate concepts.

These concepts represent patterns emerging from the evidence rather than conclusions derived from individual studies or opinions.

Concepts remain provisional until validated.

---

# Validation

Validation confirms that a concept is:

- Supported by multiple independent sources.
- Broadly applicable.
- Consistent with the Sovereign Vision.
- Suitable for informing governance or presentation.

Only validated concepts should progress into Governance Principles or Presentation Standards.

---

# Knowledge Presentation Principles

Validated concepts may be promoted into enduring Knowledge Presentation Principles.

Principles define the philosophical guidance that informs future Presentation Standards while remaining independent of specific technologies or implementations.

---

# Presentation Standards

Presentation Standards translate principles into consistent implementation guidance.

Standards describe how principles should be applied while allowing implementations to evolve over time.

---

# Implementation

Implementation applies approved standards through documentation, applications, user interfaces, intelligent tools, or other technologies.

Implementation should remain flexible while maintaining alignment with Governance, Principles, and Standards.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Research is not considered permanently complete.

As new evidence, technologies, and practical experience emerge, research should be revisited to:

- Validate existing concepts.
- Refine previous conclusions.
- Introduce new concepts where appropriate.
- Improve future standards and implementations.

Continuous improvement is an intended feature of the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

# Governance

The Research Methodology should evolve where practical experience identifies opportunities to improve research quality, transparency, or repeatability.

Methodological changes should remain aligned with the Sovereign Research Philosophy and be supported by evidence wherever practical.
