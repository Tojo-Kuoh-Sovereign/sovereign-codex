# Knowledge Presentation Research – Methodology Summary

## Research Philosophy

The purpose of Knowledge Presentation Research is not to catalogue academic literature or promote individual theories. Instead, it exists to identify enduring concepts that are consistently supported by credible evidence and translate those concepts into principles that improve how knowledge is communicated.

Research should remain evidence-informed, transparent, and open to refinement. As new evidence becomes available, existing concepts, principles, and standards should be reviewed and improved where appropriate.

Continuous improvement is a feature of the Sovereign Codex, not a flaw.

---

## Research Workflow

Every concept follows the same evidence-based workflow:

Research Question

↓

Evidence Register

↓

Evidence-Based Concept

↓

Validation

↓

Knowledge Presentation Principle

↓

Presentation Standard

↓

Implementation

```

Each stage must justify the next.

* **Research Question** defines what we are trying to understand.
* **Evidence Register** records supporting evidence from multiple independent and credible sources.
* **Evidence-Based Concept** captures the recurring idea emerging from the evidence.
* **Validation** confirms that the concept is consistently supported, broadly applicable, and aligned with the Sovereign mission.
* **Knowledge Presentation Principle** defines the enduring philosophy.
* **Presentation Standard** describes how the principle should be applied.
* **Implementation** applies the standard using a specific platform or technology.

This ensures that standards are never created from opinion, and principles are never created from a single source.

---

## Evidence Register

The Evidence Register exists to capture recurring concepts rather than individual opinions.

A concept should generally be supported by:

* Two or more independent, credible sources.
* Preference for peer-reviewed research, systematic reviews, academic texts, or recognised organisations.
* Evidence from different authors or disciplines where possible.
* Conflicting evidence should be recorded rather than ignored.

The objective is not to collect more research, but to identify recurring patterns that consistently emerge across independent sources.

---

## Guiding Statement

> We do not build standards from opinions, and we do not build principles from a single study. We build them from concepts that consistently emerge from credible evidence.

---

## Current Research Questions

The current Knowledge Presentation research programme focuses on four fundamental questions:

1. **How do people discover information?**
2. **How do people develop understanding?**
3. **What makes knowledge trustworthy?**
4. **What helps people retain and apply knowledge?**

These questions form the foundation for all future Knowledge Presentation Principles.

---

## Current Concept Example

### Information Scent

**Research Question**

How do people discover information?

**Evidence-Based Concept**

People make navigation decisions using small cues that help them predict whether a path will lead to the information they are seeking.

These cues include navigation labels, headings, search results, descriptions, and surrounding context.

**Validation**

Supported by independent research in:

* Information Foraging Theory
* Human-Computer Interaction
* Web Usability
* Search Interface Research

**Current Status**

Validated as an Evidence-Based Concept.

Further related concepts (such as Recognition vs. Recall and Scanning Behaviour) should be researched before promoting this into a Knowledge Presentation Principle.

---

## Research Governance

Knowledge Presentation Research is intended to be a living body of knowledge.

Research is never considered permanently complete.

As new evidence, technologies, and understanding emerge, concepts should be revisited, validated, refined, or, where necessary, replaced.

The objective is not to defend previous decisions, but to continually improve the quality, accessibility, and trustworthiness of knowledge presented throughout the Sovereign Codex.

Continuous refinement is fundamental to the mission of transforming information into trusted knowledge through governance, structure, and intelligent tools.
