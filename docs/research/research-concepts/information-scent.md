Concept 01 — Information Scent
Evidence Register
Source 1 — Information Foraging Theory (Pirolli & Card)

The theory proposes that people make navigation decisions by estimating whether a particular path is likely to lead to valuable information, balancing the expected value against the effort required. Users abandon a path when the expected value decreases.

Source 2 — Jakob Nielsen / Nielsen Norman Group

Nielsen applied Information Foraging Theory to web usability, arguing that users rely on labels, links, headings, navigation, and surrounding context to predict whether they will find the information they seek. When those cues are misleading, users quickly lose trust and abandon the path.

Source 3 — ACM Research (Loumakis et al.)

Research on search results found that text provides the strongest information scent, while combining text and images can strengthen users' ability to judge relevance. Images alone were generally weaker cues than descriptive text.

Evidence-Based Concept

People rarely inspect every available option.

Instead they evaluate small cues that help predict:

Will this contain what I'm looking for?
Is it worth my time?
Should I continue or try another path?

Those cues become the basis for navigation.
