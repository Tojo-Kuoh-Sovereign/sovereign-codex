# Review Process

The Review Process defines the workflow used to evaluate, refine, and publish documentation within Sovereign Codex.

Its purpose is to ensure that information is reviewed for accuracy, consistency, and quality before it becomes part of the project's verified knowledge base.

The process is intended to support continuous improvement while maintaining the integrity of published documentation.

---

## Documentation Workflow

Documentation progresses through the following lifecycle:

1. Research
2. Draft
3. Review
4. Approval
5. Publication
6. Ongoing Maintenance

Each stage contributes to improving the quality and reliability of the final documentation.

---

## Research

Information is gathered from available evidence sources, including in-game observation, official communications, and verified community research.

Where uncertainty exists, it should be identified during this stage.

---

## Draft

Information is organised into a structured document that follows the project's documentation standards.

Drafts should focus on clarity, completeness, and logical organisation.

---

## Review

Documentation is reviewed for:

- Technical accuracy
- Consistency with project standards
- Supporting evidence
- Readability
- Internal links and references

Where necessary, revisions are made before publication.

---

## Approval

Once the documentation satisfies the project's governance and documentation standards, it may be approved for publication.

Approval indicates that the document represents the best available information at the time of publication.

---

## Publication

Approved documentation becomes part of the Knowledge section of Sovereign Codex.

Published documentation should remain accessible, maintainable, and appropriately linked to related content.

---

## Ongoing Maintenance

Publication is not considered the final stage of the documentation lifecycle.

As Black Desert Online evolves, documentation should be reviewed and updated to reflect new evidence, gameplay changes, or improvements in understanding.

Continuous maintenance is an essential part of maintaining a trusted knowledge base.
