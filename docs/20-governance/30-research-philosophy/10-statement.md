# Purpose

The Research Philosophy defines the guiding beliefs that underpin research throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Its purpose is not to prescribe research methods or workflows, but to establish the enduring philosophy that guides how research should be approached, interpreted, and applied.

The philosophy provides the foundation upon which the Research Methodology, Knowledge Presentation Principles, Decision Framework, and future Presentation Standards are built.

---

# Research Philosophy

The purpose of research within the Sovereign Documentation Framework is not to catalogue academic literature or promote individual theories.

Research exists to identify enduring concepts that consistently emerge from credible evidence and transform those concepts into principles that improve how knowledge is created, communicated, and maintained.

Research should remain evidence-informed, transparent, objective, and open to continual refinement.

As new evidence becomes available, existing concepts, principles, and standards should be reviewed and improved where appropriate.

Continuous improvement is an intentional characteristic of the Sovereign Documentation Framework rather than a response to failure.

---

# Research Values

Research throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework is guided by the following values.

## Evidence-Informed

Research should be informed by credible evidence rather than individual opinion, assumption, or preference.

Enduring concepts should emerge from recurring patterns observed across multiple independent and credible sources.

---

## Objectivity

Research should seek understanding before conclusions.

Questions should be investigated openly, and conclusions should remain proportionate to the available evidence.

Where evidence changes, conclusions should also be prepared to change.

---

## Transparency

The reasoning behind research conclusions should be understandable and appropriately documented.

Where uncertainty exists, it should be acknowledged rather than hidden.

Conflicting evidence should be documented where it provides meaningful context.

---

## Governance

Research exists to support Governance.

Its purpose is to provide a trustworthy foundation for Principles, Standards, and implementation decisions rather than produce isolated academic knowledge.

Research should strengthen the long-term quality and integrity of the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Research is never considered permanently complete.

As knowledge, technology, and practical experience evolve, research should also evolve.

The objective is not to defend previous conclusions but to continually improve the quality, accessibility, maintainability, and trustworthiness of knowledge.

---

# Guiding Statement

> **We do not build standards from opinions, and we do not build principles from a single study. We build them from concepts that consistently emerge from credible evidence.**

# Governance

The Research Philosophy is intended to remain stable over time.

Changes should occur only where they strengthen the long-term governance of research within the Sovereign Documentation Framework while remaining aligned with the Governance Vision.

Operational research processes are defined separately within the Research Methodology.
