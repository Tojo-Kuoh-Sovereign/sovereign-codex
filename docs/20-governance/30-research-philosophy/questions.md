# Purpose

Research Questions define the scope and direction of investigation throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Their purpose is to identify meaningful questions that, when explored through structured research, contribute to a deeper understanding of how knowledge should be governed, presented, maintained, and applied.

Research Questions provide the starting point for evidence collection, concept development, validation, and the creation of evidence-informed principles and standards.

---

# Statement

Research begins with a question.

Rather than assuming solutions, the Sovereign Documentation Framework seeks to understand problems through deliberate investigation.

Research Questions establish the boundaries of inquiry by defining what the framework is attempting to understand before evidence is gathered or conclusions are reached.

Well-defined questions promote focused research, reduce bias, improve traceability, and ensure that findings remain aligned with the objectives of the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

---

# Principles

## Curiosity Before Conclusions

Research should begin with genuine questions rather than predetermined answers.

Questions should encourage investigation rather than confirm existing assumptions.

---

## Purposeful Investigation

Research Questions should address meaningful problems that contribute to improving governance, knowledge presentation, user understanding, or framework development.

Questions should have a clear purpose, practical value, and an identifiable outcome.

---

## Evidence Before Principles

Research Questions do not establish principles.

Their purpose is to guide evidence collection from which enduring concepts and principles may later emerge.

---

## Clear Scope

Each Research Question should define a focused area of investigation.

Well-scoped questions improve the quality of evidence collection, reduce unnecessary complexity, and make research easier to review.

---

## Continuous Discovery

Research Questions may evolve as understanding improves.

New questions should emerge naturally through implementation experience, validated concepts, technological change, and ongoing framework development.

# Governance

Research Questions should remain objective, purposeful, and aligned with the Research Philosophy.

Questions should not assume conclusions, prescribe solutions, or introduce implementation decisions before evidence has been evaluated.

Research Questions may be refined where doing so improves the clarity, relevance, or effectiveness of the investigation.
