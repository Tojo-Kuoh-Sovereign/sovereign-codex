# Purpose

The Research Documents Standard defines the required structure, responsibilities, and presentation of Research documentation throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Its purpose is to ensure Research documents consistently communicate evidence, observations, concepts, and validated knowledge while remaining transparent, traceable, maintainable, and evidence-informed.

---

# Relationships

This standard implements the following Governance Principles:

- Accuracy
- Transparency
- Maintainability
- Continuous Improvement

This standard supports:

- Research Philosophy
- Research Methodology
- Governance
- Knowledge Presentation Principles
- Decision Framework

---

# Standard

Research documents exist to record, organise, and communicate the process of investigation.

They should document evidence, observations, concepts, and validated understanding without introducing governance decisions or implementation guidance.

Research documentation should remain objective, evidence-informed, traceable, and open to refinement as additional information becomes available.

---

# Canonical Structure

Research documents should normally follow the following structure:

1. Metadata
2. Purpose
3. Relationships
4. Research Content
5. Governance
6. Version History

Additional sections may be included where they improve clarity or are required by the document's purpose.

The canonical structure promotes consistency across Research documentation while allowing flexibility for different research artefacts.

---

# Research Artefacts

Research documentation may include a variety of artefact types throughout the research lifecycle.

Examples include:

- Research Questions
- Evidence Registers
- Research Concepts
- Validated Concepts
- Research Methodology

Each artefact should communicate a single responsibility while remaining aligned with the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

---

# Evidence

Research documents should clearly distinguish between:

- Evidence
- Observation
- Interpretation
- Concept
- Validation

Where appropriate, conclusions should remain directly traceable to the supporting evidence from which they were derived.

Evidence should be presented as objectively as practical, with interpretation clearly distinguished from observation.

---

# Traceability

Research should remain transparent throughout its lifecycle.

Where practical, readers should be able to understand:

- What question was investigated.
- What evidence was collected.
- How observations informed concepts.
- Why concepts were validated.
- How validated concepts informed Governance, Principles, or Standards.

Traceability supports confidence, review, maintainability, and future refinement.

---

# Governance

Research documentation should evolve through continuous investigation and evidence-informed refinement.

Changes should improve understanding while remaining aligned with the Research Philosophy and Research Methodology.

Research documents should avoid presenting assumptions, preferences, or unvalidated interpretations as established knowledge.
