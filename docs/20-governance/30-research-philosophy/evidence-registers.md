# Purpose

Evidence Registers provide a structured record of the evidence collected throughout research conducted within the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Their purpose is to organise observations, academic literature, industry guidance, implementation experience, and other credible sources in a transparent, traceable, and maintainable manner.

Evidence Registers support objective research by clearly separating evidence collection from interpretation, validation, and conclusion.

---

# Statement

Evidence should be gathered before conclusions are reached.

The Sovereign Documentation Framework uses Evidence Registers to document the information considered during research while preserving transparency regarding its origin, relevance, reliability, and context.

Evidence Registers do not establish principles, governance positions, or standards.

Instead, they provide the evidential foundation from which recurring concepts may be identified, validated, and ultimately inform Governance, Principles, and Standards.

---

# Principles

## Evidence Before Interpretation

Evidence should be recorded independently of conclusions wherever practical.

Maintaining this separation reduces bias, improves transparency, and preserves the integrity of the research process.

---

## Transparency

Evidence should identify its source wherever practical.

Readers should be able to understand where information originated, how it was collected, and how it contributed to the research process.

---

## Diverse Sources

Evidence may be gathered from a variety of credible sources appropriate to the subject being investigated, including:

- Official documentation and announcements
- Direct observation and testing
- Experimental results
- Community knowledge and discussion
- Established reference material
- Practical implementation and operational experience

The credibility, relevance, and limitations of evidence should always be considered within the context of the research question.

No single source should normally determine a research outcome.

---

## Traceability

Evidence should remain traceable throughout the research lifecycle.

Research Concepts, Validated Concepts, Principles, and Standards should all be capable of referencing the evidence that informed their development.

---

## Continuous Refinement

Evidence Registers are living research artefacts.

As new evidence becomes available, registers should be reviewed, expanded, and refined to improve the quality, completeness, and reliability of the research.

# Governance

Evidence Registers should remain objective, transparent, and evidence-informed.

Their purpose is to document available evidence rather than advocate for particular conclusions, governance positions, or implementation decisions.

Interpretation and validation should occur separately through Research Concepts and Validated Concepts.
