# Purpose

Research Concepts capture recurring ideas, observations, and patterns that emerge during investigation throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Their purpose is to organise developing understanding before sufficient evidence has accumulated to support validation.

Research Concepts provide the intermediate stage between evidence collection and validated knowledge.

---

# Statement

Research rarely produces immediate conclusions.

As evidence is gathered, recurring themes, relationships, and observations begin to emerge.

Research Concepts provide a structured way to document these emerging patterns while recognising that further investigation and validation may still be required.

They represent developing understanding rather than established knowledge and should remain open to refinement as additional evidence becomes available.

---

# Principles

## Emergent Understanding

Research Concepts should represent patterns that emerge naturally from collected evidence rather than predetermined conclusions.

Concepts should develop through investigation rather than assumption.

---

## Evidence-Informed

Every Research Concept should be supported by relevant evidence documented within one or more Evidence Registers.

Concepts should remain traceable to the evidence that informed their development.

---

## Working Knowledge

Research Concepts represent the current understanding of a topic.

They are intended to support ongoing investigation rather than establish Governance, Principles, Standards, or implementation guidance.

---

## Open to Revision

Research Concepts should remain flexible.

As new evidence is gathered, concepts may be refined, merged, expanded, validated, or rejected.

Revision is an expected and valuable part of the research process.

---

## Bridge to Validation

Research Concepts provide the transition between evidence collection and validated knowledge.

Only concepts that demonstrate sufficient confidence through recurring evidence should progress to Validated Concepts.

---

# Knowledge State

Within the Sovereign Documentation Framework, Research Concepts represent **developing knowledge**.

They provide a structured mechanism for recording emerging understanding without implying that conclusions are final or universally accepted.

This distinction allows research to progress transparently while preserving a clear separation between evidence, concepts, validated knowledge, and governance.

---

# Relationships

This document supports:

- Research Methodology
- Research Questions
- Evidence Registers
- Validated Concepts
- Knowledge Presentation Principles

Research Concepts organise emerging understanding and support the transition from evidence to validated knowledge.

---

# Governance

Research Concepts should remain objective, transparent, and evidence-informed.

Their purpose is to capture emerging understanding rather than establish enduring principles, governance positions, or implementation guidance.

Concepts should be reviewed as additional evidence becomes available and either refined, validated, merged, or retired where appropriate.
