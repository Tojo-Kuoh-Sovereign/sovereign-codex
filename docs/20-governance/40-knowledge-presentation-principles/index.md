# Purpose

The Knowledge Presentation Principles define the enduring philosophy for communicating knowledge throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

These principles are evidence-informed, technology-independent, and intended to remain stable over time. They define **why** knowledge should be presented in particular ways rather than prescribing specific implementation techniques.

Presentation Standards, design decisions, documentation practices, and intelligent tools should derive from these principles.

# Principles

## Principle 1 — Purpose Before Presentation

> **Knowledge exists to help people achieve an outcome, not simply to present information.**

Knowledge should support meaningful objectives such as:

- Answering questions
- Solving problems
- Completing tasks
- Supporting learning
- Reducing uncertainty

Presentation should always serve the user's objective rather than the presentation medium.

---

## Principle 2 — Progressive Engagement

> **Knowledge should support progressive engagement by allowing users to rapidly evaluate relevance before requiring sustained attention.**

Users rarely begin by reading in detail.

Knowledge should support gradual investment of attention by helping users determine relevance before presenting detailed information.

---

## Principle 3 — Respect the User's Current Understanding

> **Knowledge should meet people where they are, not where the system expects them to be.**

People interact with knowledge using their existing understanding, vocabulary, goals, and mental models.

Knowledge presentation should support users in progressing from their current understanding without assuming prior expertise.

---

## Principle 4 — Reduce Unnecessary Cognitive Effort

> **Knowledge should minimise unnecessary cognitive effort while preserving meaningful challenge.**

Users should devote cognitive effort to understanding knowledge rather than interpreting presentation.

Presentation should reduce avoidable cognitive load through clear organisation, language, hierarchy, and structure.

---

## Principle 5 — Preserve Orientation

> **Knowledge should help users understand where they are, how information relates, and where they can go next.**

Knowledge rarely exists in isolation.

Presentation should preserve context, relationships, and navigation so users can confidently move throughout a broader body of knowledge.

---

## Principle 6 — Support Knowledge Progression

> **Knowledge should support movement from uncertainty towards understanding through progressive learning.**

Understanding develops over time.

Knowledge presentation should recognise different stages of learning while supporting continued progression where appropriate.

---

## Principle 7 — Build Confidence Through Clarity

> **Knowledge should communicate clearly enough that users can confidently judge relevance, credibility, and applicability.**

Clear communication supports trust.

Users should be able to determine whether information is appropriate for their current objective without unnecessary ambiguity.

---

## Principle 8 — Encourage Meaningful Exploration

> **Knowledge should reward curiosity without interrupting understanding.**

Related knowledge should be discoverable through meaningful relationships rather than arbitrary navigation.

Exploration should enhance learning while respecting the user's current objective.

---

## Principle 9 — Continuous Improvement

> **Knowledge presentation is an evolving discipline informed by research, implementation, observation, and user experience.**

Knowledge Presentation Principles should remain stable.

Presentation Standards and implementations should evolve as:

- New evidence emerges.
- Technology evolves.
- User behaviour changes.
- Better implementation approaches are identified.

Continuous improvement is an intentional characteristic of the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

# Scope

These principles apply to all knowledge communicated throughout the Sovereign ecosystem, including:

- Knowledge Articles
- Documentation
- Tutorials
- Search Experiences
- Navigation Systems
- AI Assistants
- Intelligent Tools
- Applications
- APIs

---

# Relationship to Implementation

These principles intentionally avoid prescribing implementation details.

Examples include:

- Page layouts
- Cards
- Tables
- Accordions
- Breadcrumbs
- Inline links
- Search interfaces
- AI interactions

Implementation decisions belong within Presentation Standards and should always be evaluated against the principles defined within this document.

# Governance

The Knowledge Presentation Principles are informed by the Research Philosophy, validated through the Research Methodology, and refined through Continuous Improvement.

Principles should emerge from validated concepts supported by credible evidence rather than personal preference, convention, or individual studies.

Changes should occur only where research demonstrates improved approaches to communicating knowledge while remaining aligned with the Governance Vision.

---

# Review

These principles should be reviewed periodically alongside:

- Research Questions
- Evidence Registers
- Validated Concepts
- Presentation Standards
- Implementation Experience

The objective is not to preserve historical decisions, but to continually improve the quality, accessibility, maintainability, and trustworthiness of knowledge throughout the Sovereign ecosystem.
