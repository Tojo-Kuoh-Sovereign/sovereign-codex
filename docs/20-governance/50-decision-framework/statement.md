# Purpose

The Decision Framework establishes a consistent approach for evaluating significant decisions throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that governance decisions remain transparent, evidence-informed, and aligned with the long-term vision of the Sovereign Codex.

The Decision Framework promotes deliberate decision-making while supporting continuous improvement as the framework evolves.

---

# Overview

Every significant decision made within the Sovereign Documentation Framework should be guided by evidence, governance, and long-term sustainability rather than convenience or personal preference.

The Decision Framework provides a consistent set of questions that help evaluate proposals before they become principles, standards, or implementations.

Its objective is not to restrict innovation, but to encourage thoughtful, well-governed decision-making.

---

# Decision Principles

Significant decisions should be evaluated against the following principles.

## Vision Alignment

Does the proposal support the Sovereign Vision?

Every decision should contribute towards the long-term mission of transforming information into trusted knowledge through governance, structure, and intelligent tools.

---

## Governance Alignment

Is the proposal consistent with the Sovereign Governance Model?

Decisions should reinforce the framework rather than introduce unnecessary complexity or conflicting approaches.

---

## Evidence-Informed

Is the decision supported by research, practical experience, or credible evidence?

Where evidence is limited, uncertainty should be acknowledged and documented.

---

## Appropriate Responsibility

Has the proposal been placed within the correct layer of the framework?

Questions should be considered in terms of:

- Vision
- Governance
- Research
- Principles
- Standards
- Implementation

Correctly identifying the responsibility of a decision promotes clarity and reduces duplication.

---

## Long-Term Sustainability

Will this decision remain appropriate as the Sovereign Documentation Framework grows?

Decisions should favour maintainability and scalability over short-term convenience.

---

## Simplicity

Does the proposal reduce unnecessary complexity?

Simple solutions that achieve the desired outcome should generally be preferred over more complicated alternatives.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Can this decision be reviewed and refined as new knowledge or experience becomes available?

Governance should remain adaptable without compromising its core principles.

---

# Decision Lifecycle

Significant decisions generally follow an iterative lifecycle.

```text
Observation

↓

Question

↓

Research

↓

Evaluation

↓

Decision

↓

Implementation

↓

Review
```

Each stage informs the next while supporting continual refinement of the framework.

---

# Decision Outcomes

Following evaluation, proposals should normally result in one of the following outcomes.

| Outcome | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Approve** | The proposal aligns with Governance and may proceed. |
| **Refine** | Additional research or clarification is required before approval. |
| **Defer** | The proposal has value but is postponed for future consideration. |
| **Reject** | The proposal does not currently align with the Sovereign Documentation Framework. |
