# Citation Standard

The Citation Standard defines how sources should be referenced throughout Sovereign Codex.

Its purpose is to promote transparency by allowing readers to understand where information originates and how it has been supported.

Citations should provide sufficient context for readers to evaluate the reliability of documented information without unnecessarily interrupting the flow of the document.

---

## Core Principles

Citations should be:

- Transparent
- Relevant
- Consistent
- Verifiable
- Appropriate

---

## When to Cite

Sources should be cited whenever documentation:

- Presents factual information
- References official communications
- Summarises community research
- Includes historical information
- Makes technical recommendations based on supporting evidence

Widely understood information that does not benefit from attribution may not require formal citation.

---

## Citation Sources

Acceptable citations may include:

- Official Black Desert Online announcements
- Pearl Abyss patch notes
- In-game observations
- Verified community research
- Developer communications
- Project research conducted within Sovereign Codex

The reliability of each source should be considered alongside the project's Evidence Model.

---

## Citation Style

Citations should be presented consistently throughout the project.

Where practical, references should identify:

- Source
- Date (where applicable)
- Context
- Relevant supporting material

The objective is to provide readers with sufficient information to locate or verify the original source.

---

## Transparency

Documentation should clearly distinguish between:

- Verified information
- Supported conclusions
- Assumptions
- Speculation

Readers should be able to understand the confidence associated with documented information without ambiguity.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Citation practices should evolve alongside the project.

Improvements should strengthen transparency, consistency, and long-term maintainability while remaining practical for contributors.
