# Accessibility

The Accessibility system defines the principles that ensure Sovereign Codex remains usable, understandable, and inclusive for the widest possible audience.

Its purpose is to remove unnecessary barriers while preserving the clarity, consistency, and integrity of the platform.

Accessibility should be considered a fundamental aspect of good design rather than an additional feature.

---

## Purpose

Accessibility exists to improve the experience for every reader.

Clear structure, thoughtful hierarchy, and predictable interaction benefit all users regardless of device, ability, or experience.

Inclusive design produces better documentation.

---

## Accessibility Philosophy

Accessibility should be:

- Inclusive
- Readable
- Predictable
- Consistent
- Practical

Accessibility is achieved through deliberate design rather than isolated features.

---

## Readability

Documentation should support comfortable long-form reading.

Typography, spacing, colour, and hierarchy should work together to reduce cognitive effort and improve comprehension.

Knowledge should remain the focus.

---

## Navigation

Users should be able to move through the platform confidently using a variety of input methods.

Navigation should remain predictable, logical, and consistent across every discipline of the Codex.

---

## Communication

Information should never rely upon colour alone.

Meaning should be reinforced through headings, labels, icons, structure, and supporting text wherever appropriate.

Clear communication improves accessibility for everyone.

---

## Responsive Experience

The platform should remain effective across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

Responsive layouts should preserve hierarchy and readability rather than simply rearranging content.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Accessibility should evolve alongside the platform.

Improvements should strengthen usability, inclusivity, and long-term maintainability while remaining faithful to the Design Philosophy.
