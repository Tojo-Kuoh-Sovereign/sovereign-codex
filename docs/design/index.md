## One refinement I'd make

There's one tiny tweak I'd make to the phrase we both love.

Instead of:

> **A classified engineering archive belonging to a royal order.**

I'd write:

> **A premium engineering archive maintained with the discipline and permanence of a royal institution.**

Why?

Because it shifts the focus from fantasy to values.

The "royal" aspect becomes symbolic—it represents stewardship, longevity, and high standards rather than crowns and castles.

That fits perfectly with the architecture we've built. Someone should visit Sovereign Codex and think:

> "These people care deeply about getting things right."

If the design communicates that before they've read a single article, then we've succeeded.

And I have to say... this is one of the most enjoyable design exercises I've been part of. We're not choosing colours because they look nice—we're choosing them because they reinforce the identity of the platform. That's exactly how enduring brands are built.
