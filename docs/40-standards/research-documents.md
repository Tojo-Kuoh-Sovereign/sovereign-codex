# Purpose

The Research Documents Standard defines the structure, responsibilities, and presentation of Research documentation throughout the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

Its purpose is to ensure Research documents consistently communicate evidence, observations, concepts, and validated knowledge while remaining transparent, traceable, and maintainable.

---

# Relationships

This standard implements the following Governance principles:

- Accuracy
- Transparency
- Maintainability
- Continuous Improvement

This standard supports:

- Research Philosophy
- Research Methodology
- Governance
- Standards
- Knowledge

---

# Standard

Research documents exist to record and communicate the process of investigation.

They should document evidence, observations, concepts, and validated understanding without introducing unnecessary implementation guidance or governance decisions.

Research documentation should remain objective, evidence-informed, and open to refinement as additional information becomes available.

---

# Canonical Structure

Research documents should normally follow the following structure:

1. Metadata
2. Purpose
3. Relationships
4. Research Content
5. Governance
6. Version History

Additional sections may be included where they improve clarity or are required by the responsibility of the document.

---

# Research Artefacts

Research documentation may include a variety of artefact types throughout the research lifecycle.

Examples include:

- Research Questions
- Evidence Registers
- Research Concepts
- Validated Concepts
- Research Methodology

Each artefact should communicate a single responsibility while remaining consistent with the Sovereign Documentation Framework.

---

# Evidence

Research documents should clearly distinguish between:

- Evidence
- Observation
- Interpretation
- Concept
- Validation

Where appropriate, conclusions should remain traceable to the supporting evidence from which they were derived.

---

# Traceability

Research should remain transparent throughout its lifecycle.

Where practical, readers should be able to understand:

- What question was investigated.
- What evidence was collected.
- How concepts emerged.
- Why concepts were validated.
- How research influenced Governance or Standards.

Traceability supports confidence, review, and future refinement.

---

# Governance

Research documentation should evolve through continuous investigation and evidence-informed refinement.

Changes should improve understanding while remaining aligned with the Sovereign Research Philosophy and Research Methodology.

Research documents should avoid presenting assumptions or personal preferences as validated knowledge.
